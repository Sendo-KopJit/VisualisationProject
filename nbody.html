Newton's Law of Gravitation

Every pair of masses exerts an attractive force along the line joining them. The magnitude is proportional to the product of the masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Fij = G * mi * mj / rij2
Ggravitational constant (scaled for this simulation) mi, mjmasses of bodies i and j rijdistance between body i and body j

For N bodies the net force on body i is the vector sum of Fij over all j not equal to i. A softening term (epsilon squared) is added to rij2 to prevent singularities at close range. The equations of motion are integrated with a 4th-order Runge-Kutta scheme, sub-stepped at high time scales for stability.

Conservation Laws

The total mechanical energy E = K + U is conserved. So is the total linear momentum. Watch the dE/E0 readout: a well-tuned integrator keeps drift below 1%. If it climbs, reduce the time scale.

K = (1/2) * sum( mi * vi2 )
U = -sum( G * mi * mj / rij )   for all i < j

Lagrange Points

In a two-body system, five equilibrium positions exist where a third small body remains stationary in the co-rotating frame. L1, L2, and L3 lie on the line connecting the two primaries and are unstable. L4 and L5 sit at the apex of equilateral triangles formed with the two primaries and are stable: a perturbed body will oscillate around them rather than escape.

L1, L2, L3collinear points unstable L4, L5triangular points at +/- 60 deg from secondary stable

Jupiter's Trojan asteroids occupy the Sun-Jupiter L4 and L5 points. Over 10,000 have been catalogued.

What to look for

  • Figure-8: All three equal-mass bodies trace a single figure-of-eight. This is a choreographic solution found by Chenciner and Montgomery in 2000 and is remarkably stable.
  • Solar system: Inner planets orbit faster. This follows Kepler's third law: orbital period scales with the 3/2 power of the semi-major axis. Crank the time scale to watch outer planets crawl.
  • Chaotic 3-body: The system is exquisitely sensitive to initial conditions. Tiny numerical differences compound into entirely different trajectories. This is deterministic chaos, not randomness.
  • Lagrange points: Enable the overlay toggle. The L4 and L5 test particles librate stably inside the dashed circles. Try adding a small body nearby to see it get captured or ejected.
  • Slingshot: The small probe arrives on a hyperbolic trajectory and exits faster than it arrived, having stolen a fraction of the planet's orbital energy.
